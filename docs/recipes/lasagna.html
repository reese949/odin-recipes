Classic Lasagna Recipe

A delicious and comforting lasagna made with layers of pasta, meat sauce, and creamy cheese.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or pork, or a mix of both)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz or 800g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz or 170g) tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce (optional for a smoother sauce)
  • 2 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp pepper (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, helps balance acidity)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

For the Cheese Layer:

  • 15 oz (425g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups (200g) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Lasagna:

  • 12-15 lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil noodles)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (for topping)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (for topping)
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:

  1. Cook the Ground Beef: Heat a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until it is browned and fully cooked, about 6-8 minutes. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: Add the chopped onion to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Seasoning: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce (if using), dried basil, oregano, salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed, and add fresh parsley towards the end for extra flavor.

2. Prepare the Cheese Mixture:

  1. Mix Ricotta, Egg, and Cheese: In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, shredded mozzarella (reserving some for topping), grated Parmesan, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together until smooth and well-mixed. This will be your creamy cheese layer.

3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:

  1. Boil the Noodles: If you're using regular lasagna noodles (not no-boil), bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions, usually about 8-10 minutes, until al dente.
  2. Drain the noodles and lay them out flat on a clean kitchen towel or parchment paper to prevent them from sticking together.

4. Assemble the Lasagna:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Layer the Lasagna: In a 9x13 inch (23x33 cm) baking dish, start by spreading a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom. Add a layer of cooked lasagna noodles (about 3-4 noodles, depending on the size of your dish). Spread a third of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles. Add a layer of meat sauce over the cheese. Repeat the process: noodles, ricotta cheese, meat sauce. Continue layering until you've used up all the ingredients, finishing with a layer of meat sauce on top.

5. Add the Toppings:

  1. Top the Lasagna: Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese and Parmesan cheese over the top of the lasagna.
  2. Cover with Foil: Cover the baking dish loosely with aluminum foil. This helps the lasagna cook evenly and prevents the cheese from burning.

6. Bake the Lasagna:

  1. Bake Covered: Place the lasagna in the preheated oven and bake for 25 minutes.
  2. Uncover and Bake Again: After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

7. Rest and Serve:

  1. Cool and Set: After removing from the oven, let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes. This allows the layers to set and makes it easier to cut.
  2. Serve: Slice the lasagna into squares and serve with fresh basil or parsley on top, if desired.

Tips for the Best Lasagna

  • Make Ahead: Lasagna can be made a day ahead. Just assemble it, cover with foil, and refrigerate. Bake it the next day for an additional 20-25 minutes.
  • Freeze for Later: You can freeze lasagna either before or after baking. If freezing before baking, cover tightly with foil and freeze for up to 3 months. Bake directly from the freezer (add about 25-30 minutes to the cooking time).
  • Meat Variations: You can use ground turkey, chicken, or even a meatless version with sautéed vegetables like zucchini, mushrooms, and spinach.
